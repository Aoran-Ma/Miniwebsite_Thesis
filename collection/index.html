Here's a compilation of vintage LGBTQ typography sourced from liberation publications spanning the 1960s to the 1980s. This collection includes notable publications such as THE GAY ALTERNATIVE, a non-profit gay magazine published by the Gay Alternative Collective, as well as Gay Liberator in Detroit, Come Out! - a liberation forum for the gay community in Philadelphia, and NewGayLife, also in Philadelphia. Throughout my research, I found immense appreciation for the diverse range of typographical styles utilized by editors during this era. They fearlessly employed multiple typefaces, whether to convey expressive statements or simply create engaging and colorful advertisements. Despite the limitations in printing techniques at the time, the creativity stemming from the desire to challenge normative structures remained unburied; instead, it flourished vibrantly.
